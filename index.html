The wedge

Start where the need is obvious.

Small farms and farm-learning programs run on spoken context: what happened in the bed, who saw it, what changed after the rain, what a mentor said, what should happen next. Most software turns that living knowledge into fragile forms and forgotten dashboards.

Build a Farm starts as a simple field companion: ask a question, capture a note, preserve the context, and turn it into an owned record the farm can use later.

Farm records

Voice-first logs for observations, crop state, water, pests, harvest, and lessons learned.

Cohort learning

A natural fit for farmer training, incubator programs, mentors, volunteers, and stewardship groups.

Owned continuity

Exportable records and receipts so the farm keeps its memory even if vendors change.

The company

Zenfold is not one app.

Zenfold Labs builds the substrate underneath products that need trust: local AI, consent-aware memory, witness receipts, and human-readable exports.

Build a Farm is the first public surface because the farm use case is concrete, useful, and grounded in Hawaii. The same foundation extends to other high-trust products, including protected introductions and community coordination.

Consent by design

Every record knows who it belongs to, what tier it lives in, and what can be shared.

Local intelligence

AI helps at the edge first, with cloud as optional reach rather than the center of gravity.

Receipts over vibes

Actions leave durable proof so operators, mentors, and partners can trust the history.
